The followings are the place holder for overlay text field :
$D - file directory/folder
$F - file name (with extension)
$N - file name (without extension)
$O - file extension
$U - current system date/time
$t - exif date/time
$i - ISO
$l - Focal length
$s - Shutter speed
$f - Aperture/f-stop
$h - Flash (On/Off)
$c - Camera Model
$$ - add one '$' character
Date format usage example:
$t{yyyy-MM-dd} ==> 2017-12-31 if it is the last day of 2017
Date Format
d - The day of the month, from 1 through 31.
dd - The day of the month, from 01 through 31.
ddd - The abbreviated name of the day of the week, eg, Mon.
dddd - The full name of the day of the week, eg, Monday.
h - The hour, using a 12-hour clock from 1 to 12.
hh - The hour, using a 12-hour clock from 01 to 12.
tt - The AM/PM designator.
H - The hour, using a 24-hour clock from 0 to 23.
HH - The hour, using a 24-hour clock from 00 to 23.
m - The minute, from 0 through 59.
mm - The minute, from 00 through 59.
M - The month, from 1 through 12.
MM - The month, from 01 through 12.
MMM - The abbreviated name of the month, eg, Jan.
MMMM - The full name of the month, eg, January.
s - The second, from 0 through 59.
ss - The second, from 00 through 59.
y - The year, from 0 to 99.
yy - The year, from 00 to 99.
yyyy - The year as a four-digit number.
: - The time separator.
/ - The date separator.
